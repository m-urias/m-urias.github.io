Teaching Philosophy

All my courses focus on delivering foundational economic theories in order to cultivate within students an ability to think critically "like an economist." It is easy, especially as we learn more, to get lost in the mathematical minutiae of economic models or develop a myopic focus on the tools of the trade rather than seeking true insight. I continually emphasis core principles from economic theory to help students navigate these waters. More recently, I've devoted more class time to show students how economic data validates or refutes said theories; providing code to pull data from popular databases (e.g. World Bank, FRED), visualize data, and interpret the data through economic theory. My goal is to strike a balance between grounding thought in rigorous theory, interpreting real-world economic news, and developing technical tools to understand economic data.
